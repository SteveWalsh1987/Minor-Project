How To Crack and Beat an Egg

Hold the egg with your dominant hand (right if you are right handed, left if you are left handed). Hold it at the widest part of the egg, the middle. Tap the egg against the edge of a bowl until it cracks. With your other thumb push gently into the crack until the shell separates. Open the egg shell and drop the egg into a bowl. Whisk the egg, flicking the egg forwards and backwards until the yolk and white are well mixed.

How to Line a Tin

Greasing and lining a tine ensures that your baking does not stick to the tin when it is cooked. Place the cake tin on a sheet of baking paper and trace its outline. Cut out the tracing and make sure it fits on the bottom of the baking tin.

How to rease a tin

Greasing and lining a tine ensures that your baking does not stick to the tin when it is cooked. To grease the tin, you have to put about a teaspoon of butter in your hand and rub the bottom and sides of the tin with the butter.

Sifting Flour

Sifting flour means that there is plenty of air in it and makes sure our baking is light and fluffy. It also helps to remove any lumps from the flour. Hold a sieve over a mixing bowl and pour the flour into it. Very gently tap the edge of the sieve so that the flour falls lightly into the bowl.
